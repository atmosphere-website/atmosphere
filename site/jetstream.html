The Jetstream

What is the Jetstream?

The jetstream is a current of air that flows around the arctic. You can think of the jetstream as a river of air that flows east around the world. It is kept up in the arctic by the temperature difference between the arctic and the equator, which prevents the jetstream from swinging down to the south.

The Problem

As the climate heats up, the temperature difference is decreasing, and the jetstream is swinging futher south. When the jetstream swings south, it causes violent weather like hurricanes and typhoons.
