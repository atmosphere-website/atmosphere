What are Clouds?

Clouds are made up of water vapour that has condensed into a visible cloud. Clouds are incredibly important for all ecosystems, as they are an important part of the water cycle. However, the role they play in global warming is not quite so helpful.

The Problem

The clouds, and the water vapour they hold, make up 60% of all the greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. The negative heat-trapping effect outways the albedo effect they create, and they heat the planet. Water vapour in the atmosphre multiplies the humans global warming two to three times. The clouds are also part of feedback loop where the clouds heat up the ocean, evaporating water, which forms into more clouds.
